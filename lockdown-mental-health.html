Introduction

In addition to various psychological problems like depression, anxiety, and panic disorder, the COVID-19 pandemic has caused severe threats to the lives and physical health of people around the globe. (Qiu et al., 2020). Public health strategies such as isolation, reduced social contact, and hygiene practices have helped reduce infections but also increased mental health issues like anxiety and depression. (Zhou et al., 2020).

It is crucial for governments, hospitals, educational institutions, and individuals to provide psychological interventions to address mental health challenges during such stressful situations. This study aims to understand and address the psychological impact of the lockdown.

Methodology

This survey involved 440 participants from 18 countries, 32 states, and 112 cities. The survey explored various factors impacting health, such as sleep, food, exercise, stress, and relationships. Data was collected via a Google form and analyzed using Tableau, creating 22 worksheets, 3 dashboards, and 1 storyline.

Key categories included:

  • Demographic information (age, gender, location)
  • Work-related aspects
  • Mental state
  • Relationships

The survey ensured anonymity to encourage honest responses. Responses were converted into CSV format and visualized in Tableau for interactive analysis.

Steps for Analysis

The Tableau dashboards are designed for interactive analysis. Key features include:

  1. Clicking on fields changes charts across dashboards.
  2. Hovering over fields provides additional insights.
  3. Navigation between dashboards allows for focused analysis.

Try the interactive dashboards here: Tableau Dashboard.

Conclusion

This study provided valuable insights into the psychological impact of lockdowns. While over two-thirds of participants reported happiness, a significant portion showed signs of mental distress. It is vital to prioritize mental health and promote positivity during these challenging times.
